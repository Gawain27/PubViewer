About Publication Hub

The Mission

Publication Hub was born out of the need to bridge the gaps between authors, their publications, and the journals or conferences they have participated in. The aim is to provide a centralized platform to uncover correlations, foster collaboration, and evaluate academic contributions effectively.

Features

Currently, Publication Hub offers:

  • Comprehensive author profiles with details of their publications.
  • Data integration from renowned sources such as Google Scholar, CORE.edu, DBLP and Scimagojr.
  • Dynamic exploration of journals and conferences associated with authors and their work via a conjoined network.
  • Overviews to make approximate evaluations of authors and their research impact.

The system is designed with scalability in mind, utilizing a robust scraping framework to aggregate and associate academic information across various platforms.

Open Source Commitment

Publication Hub is fully open source and distributed under the GNU AGPL (Affero General Public License). This ensures that all expanded source code is available for anyone to access, use, and modify, fostering a collaborative and growing community.

The complete project can be consulted at the following repositories:

  • PubViewer
  • PubPersister
  • PubScraper

The Vision

While the current version of Publication Hub already provides valuable insights into academic networks, the vision is to expand its capabilities into a full-fledged academic repository. This includes:

  • Enhanced evaluation metrics for authors and research quality.
  • Seamless integration of diverse academic resources.
  • Collaboration tools to connect researchers and institutions.
  • Advanced analytics for uncovering hidden trends and connections in research.
